What does AIDE do?

There are nearly 258 million children world-wide dropped out from the school, 130 million of them girls. Due to COVID-19 pandemic, schools in 185 countries were forced to close their doors to children and caused a global education crisis. According to UNESCO and Save the Children school closures and lack of access to education present an increased rate of drop-out, where almost 10 million children may never return to school.

Our aim with AIDE project is to prevent school-dropout at an early stage. The combination of a simple punch-card system and artificial intelligence /machine learning enables to register student enrolment data, detect and predict school drop outs. In collaboration government and help organizations, AIDE can contribute to effective measures to bring children back to school.

Data collection

AIDE project assigns a unique number to each student and registers initial data of gender, age, health and travel. In the next step, a simple punch card system keeps regular record of student enrolment.

AIDE

AIDE monitors nation-wide collected information about student enrolment. Artificial intelligence enables AIDE to make precise statistical analyses and suggest specific solutions to REDD BARNA (or other help/education organizations) regarding prevention of student dropout.

Prevention measures

AIDE registers measures taken by the help organizations, local authorities and collects data about the output.

Improved measures by AIDE

Machine learning enables AIDE to suggest improved solutions to REDD BARNA.
